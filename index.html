02 How to pitch me
A 5-minute email beats a 45-minute deck.

I read everything that lands in my inbox. Here's the shortest path to a real conversation and an intro to a fund.

avg. first-reply < 72h
  1. 01

    One paragraph, one link

    What you're building, for whom, and why now. Link to a live product or a 5-slide deck — whichever shows more.

    5 min
  2. 02

    The one metric

    The single number that proves you're onto something — retention curve, unit economics, waitlist growth, a signed LOI.

    —
  3. 03

    30-minute call

    If there's fit, we do a no-slides call. I ask mean questions so the real VCs don't have to.

    30 min
  4. 04

    Intros, warm and fast

    I double-opt-in every intro. You get context; partners get conviction. Expect 2–4 intros within the week.

    1 wk
— Do send
  • A link I can click in 5 seconds
  • One specific ask (intro to X, feedback on Y)
  • A screen recording > a slide of screenshots
  • Technical founders — even if the deck is ugly
  • Weird ideas that "wouldn't fundraise"
— Don't send
  • "Quick call to explore synergies"
  • NDAs before first conversation
  • Revenue projections past year 3
  • A 40-slide deck without a product
  • "We have no competitors"
